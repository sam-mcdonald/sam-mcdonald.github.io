I am an aspiring soybean breeder and geneticist at the University of Georgia’s Institute of Plant Breeding, Genetics, and Genomics. My research in the Soybean Breeding and Genetics Lab focuses on finding genetic resistance to the soybean disease frogeye leaf spot and developing molecular tools to enable efficient marker-based selection.

I grew up on a row crop farm in the Midwest, and this is where my interest in crop science began because I saw how important good cultivars were to my family’s livelihood, as well as global food security. I want to use my skills and knowledge in plant breeding and genetics to understand more about the biological control of important crop traits, but also develop germplasm and cultivars to bring superior genetics to producers and eventually consumers.
